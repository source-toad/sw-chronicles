<h2> Pre-Hyperdrive Interstellar Travel </h2>

Before the invention of the hyperdrive, interstellar travel was only possible through limited, difficult and high-risk efforts. 

### Sleeper Ships

The most reliable form of pre-hyperdrive interstellar travel was the use of long-range sublight vessels, commonly referred to as sleeper or stasis ships.

These ships were designed for journeys lasting decades or longer, accelerating to high sublight velocities and maintaining course across vast distances. To make such travel viable, crews were placed into extended stasis, preserving them for the duration of the voyage. Early versions of these missions relied on generational crews, but advances in stasis technology allowed later expeditions to reduce the number of active personnel required.

These missions were typically one-way or exploratory in nature. Some succeeded in establishing isolated settlements, while others vanished without record. A small number of dormant or derelict vessels are believed to still drift between systems, their destinations never reached.

### Purrgil-Assisted Travel

A far less understood method of early interstellar travel involved the use of Purrgil; massive, naturally spacefaring creatures capable of entering hyperspace.

Before artificial hyperdrives were developed, certain groups learned to exploit the Purrgil’s natural migration behavior. By attaching vessels to these creatures or otherwise guiding their movement, travelers could traverse interstellar distances far more quickly than sublight travel would allow.

This method was inherently unpredictable. Purrgil migrations did not follow fixed or fully understood routes, and travelers had little control over their destination once a journey began. While some expeditions successfully reached distant systems, others emerged far from their intended target, or failed to reappear at all.

Locating Purrgil pods, surviving proximity to creatures of such scale, and securing a vessel for transit required specialized knowledge and equipment. As a result, this method was never widely adopted and remained a mystery to nearly all except those who utilised it.
